Introduction

Increasingly stringent global regulations mandate that companies provide detailed, auditable climate-related disclosures. However, the multi-faceted complexity of these long-form reports---characterized by diverse, visually rich, and unstructured content---renders traditional analysis tools ineffective for the rigorous evidence-grounding required in regulatory auditing. To address this gap, we present the ClimateRAG system, a structured processing and reasoning framework designed for automated climate disclosure analysis. The system integrates hierarchical document chunking, an agent reasoning pipeline, and a claim extractor module to produce traceable and evidence-linked outputs. Furthermore, we introduce a new dataset of 363 annotated questionâ€“answer pairs covering both single-document and multi-document reasoning scenarios. Experimental evaluation demonstrate the effectiveness of ClimateRAG in analyzing climate disclosure reports. By leveraging recent advances in Large Language Models, our system provides a practical solution to facilitate reliable and automated sustainability reporting analysis, significantly reducing manual efforts required for regulatory auditing.

ClimateRAG Overview

In summary, we have made the following key contributions:
  • We develop ClimateRAG, a system that adopts hierarchical chunking and an agent-based reasoning pipeline to enable auditable, evidence-linked, and multi-document analysis of climate disclosure reports.
  • The system provides a practical solution for reliable automated sustainability reporting analysis and significantly reduces the manual effort required for regulatory auditing.
  • We conduct an extensive evaluation across multiple embedding and language models, demonstrating that ClimateRAG consistently outperforms standard RAG baselines.
