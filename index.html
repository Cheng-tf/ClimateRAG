Introduction

Increasingly stringent global regulations demand that companies provide detailed, auditable climate-related disclosures. However, analyzing these long and visually complex reports presents a challenge for regulators and auditors who require precise evidence grounding rather than free-form answers. We present the ClimateRAG system, a structured processing and reasoning framework designed for automated climate disclosure analysis. The system integrates hierarchical document chunking, an agent reasoning pipeline, and a claim extractor module to produce traceable and evidence-linked outputs. Furthermore, we introduce a novel dataset of 367 annotated questionâ€“answer pairs covering both single-document and multi-document scenarios. Experimental evaluation results indicate that the proposed methods demonstrate strong effectiveness in analyzing climate disclosure reports. The system aims to bridge the gap between Large Language Models and the rigorous requirements of regulatory auditing, thereby supporting trustworthy sustainability reporting analysis.
